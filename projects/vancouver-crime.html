Project Overview

This dashboard explores crime statistics across Vancouver neighborhoods. It visualizes crime frequency, trends, and hotspots, offering critical insights for urban planning, law enforcement strategies, and community awareness initiatives.

Key Insights

  • Crime Hotspot: Central Business District has the highest crime rate at 175.23 per 1,000 residents.
  • Other High-Risk Areas: West End, Fairview, and Mount Pleasant also show elevated crime levels.
  • Trends: Crime rates have declined steadily from 2003 to 2017 (~63% reduction).
  • Crime-to-Population: Weak correlation (r ≈ 0.27) suggests other contributing factors.
  • Low-Crime Zones: Arbutus Ridge and Dunbar consistently report lower rates.

Business Metrics

  • Average Crime Rate: 45.3 per 1,000 residents
  • Crime Rate Reduction (2003–2017): ~63%
  • Weak correlation between population size and crime rate (r ≈ 0.27)

Recommendations

  • Boost patrols in top 5 crime-heavy neighborhoods
  • Install better street lighting and surveillance in hotspots
  • Evaluate post-2010 policies that may have influenced the drop in crime
  • Review urban design of Central Business District for safety improvements

Data Preparation Process

  1. Data Source: Public city datasets (crime incidents, population stats)
  2. Data Cleaning: Null removal, formatting, standardizing, merging on neighborhood fields
  3. Modeling: Star schema with fact table (incidents) and dimension tables (dates, neighborhoods, types)
  4. Metrics: DAX for calculating per capita rates and rankings
