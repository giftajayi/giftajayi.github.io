Project Overview

This dashboard provides a longitudinal analysis of temperature extremes across Canadian provinces from 1973 to 2023. It offers visual insight into warming trends, extreme temperature events, and provincial comparisons, useful for climate researchers, policy analysts, and educators.

Key Insights

  • Highest Temperature Recorded: 46°C in British Columbia
  • Lowest Temperature Recorded: -53.7°C in Nunavut
  • Warmest Year: 2023 — aligns with global warming trends
  • Coldest Year: 1982 — significant cold snap or anomaly
  • Regional Trends:
    • BC and Manitoba show highest max temperatures frequently
    • Newfoundland and Nunavut record consistently lower extremes
    • Ontario, Alberta, and Quebec show high variability across decades
  • Map Visual: Bing Maps heat points show temperature patterns via geo-coordinates

Business Metrics

  • Avg. National Max Temp Rise: +1.4°C (1973–2023)
  • Most Variability: Alberta — Std Dev of 8.1°C
  • Top 3 Warming Provinces: BC, ON, MB — Avg increase > +1.6°C

Recommendations

  • Invest in heat-resistant public infrastructure in high-temp provinces
  • Expand emergency alert systems and cooling centers in BC & MB
  • Design proactive climate policies using insights from warming trends
